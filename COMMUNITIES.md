# Open Source and Open Science Communities

This document lists potential communities relevant to the Open Problem Toolkit project, focusing on post-quantum cryptography, lattice-based cryptography, and open science research.

---

## üîê Cryptography & Security Communities

### Post-Quantum Cryptography

* **[PQC Forum](https://groups.google.com/a/list.nist.gov/g/pqc-forum)** - NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography Standardization discussion group
  - Focus: PQC standardization, lattice-based cryptography
  - Audience: Researchers, cryptographers, industry professionals

* **[IACR (International Association for Cryptologic Research)](https://www.iacr.org/)** - Leading cryptography research organization
  - Events: Eurocrypt, Crypto, Asiacrypt conferences
  - Resources: IACR ePrint archive for preprints
  - Community: Research-oriented, peer-reviewed publications

* **[Lattice Mailing List](https://groups.google.com/g/lattice-crypto)** - Dedicated to lattice-based cryptography
  - Focus: Lattice algorithms, reduction techniques, security analysis
  - Audience: Academic researchers and practitioners

### General Cryptography

* **[Cryptography Stack Exchange](https://crypto.stackexchange.com/)** - Q&A community for cryptography
  - Focus: Practical and theoretical cryptography questions
  - Audience: Students, developers, researchers

* **[Modern Crypto Mailing List](https://moderncrypto.org/)** - Discussions on modern cryptographic systems
  - Focus: Current developments in cryptography
  - Audience: Cryptographers and security professionals

* **[OpenSSL Community](https://www.openssl.org/community/)** - Open-source cryptographic library
  - Focus: Implementation, standards compliance
  - Audience: Developers and security engineers

## üåê Standards & Compliance Communities

### Standards Organizations

* **[NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology)](https://www.nist.gov/)**
  - Focus: PQC standardization, security standards
  - Process: Open comment periods, workshops

* **[ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 27](https://www.iso.org/committee/45306.html)** - IT Security techniques
  - Standards: ISO/IEC 18033 (encryption), ISO/IEC 28033 (homomorphic encryption)
  - Audience: Standards developers, implementers

* **[HomomorphicEncryption.org](https://homomorphicencryption.org/)** - HE standardization
  - Focus: Homomorphic encryption security standards
  - Community: Academic and industry collaboration

### Security & Privacy

* **[IEEE Computer Society](https://www.computer.org/)** - Computing and IT professional society
  - Standards: IEEE 2410 (biometric privacy)
  - Focus: Security, privacy, cryptography

## üéØ Recommended Engagement Strategy

### For Project Visibility
1. **arXiv & IACR ePrint** - Share preprints and research findings
2. **Julia Discourse** - Announce releases, seek Julia-specific feedback
3. **GitHub** - Maintain active repository with documentation
4. **Lattice Mailing List** - Engage with lattice cryptography specialists

### For Collaboration
1. **PQC Forum** - Connect with NIST standardization efforts
2. **Julia Slack/Zulip** - Real-time collaboration on implementation
3. **Open Science Framework** - Share datasets and reproducible workflows
4. **NumFOCUS** - Consider fiscal sponsorship for long-term sustainability

### For Benchmarking & Validation
1. **Lattice Challenge** - Submit and compare benchmark results
2. **Open Quantum Safe** - Integrate with liboqs for compatibility
3. **SUPERCOP** - Performance comparison with other implementations

### For Standards Compliance
1. **ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 27** - Track homomorphic encryption standards
2. **HomomorphicEncryption.org** - Align with security standard recommendations
3. **NIST** - Follow PQC standardization process
